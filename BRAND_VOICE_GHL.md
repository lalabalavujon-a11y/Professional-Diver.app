## **Language & Messaging Framework**

### **Power Words We Use**
- **Professional** (not amateur or basic)
- **Comprehensive** (not simple or limited)
- **Certified/Certification** (not course or class)
- **Master** (not learn or study)
- **Expert/Expertise** (not teacher or instructor)
- **Advanced** (not beginner or entry-level)
- **Industry-Standard** (not generic or basic)
- **Safety-Critical** (not important or recommended)

### **Technical Language Guidelines**
- Use proper diving industry terminology (NDT, LST, ALST, DMT, SAT)
- Reference industry standards (IMCA, ADCI, OSHA, ASTM)
- Include specific diving operations (surface supply, saturation, inspection)
- Maintain brand neutrality - avoid favoring specific certification bodies

### **Messaging Hierarchy**
1. **Safety & Standards** - Always lead with safety and industry compliance
2. **Professional Development** - Focus on career advancement and certification
3. **Technology & Innovation** - Highlight AI and advanced learning features
4. **Accessibility & Support** - Emphasize comprehensive support and guidance

- Maintain brand neutrality across all certification bodies
- Speak to the practical challenges diving professionals face
- Use confident, authoritative language backed by expertise

### ‚ùå **DON'T**
- Oversimplify complex diving concepts

- Ignore industry-specific terminology and standards
